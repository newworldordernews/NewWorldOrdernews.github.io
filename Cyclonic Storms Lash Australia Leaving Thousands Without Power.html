Cyclonic Storms Lash Australia, Leaving Thousands Without Power

A powerful storm system, previously classified as a tropical cyclone, has battered parts of Queensland, Australia, leaving thousands of residents without electricity and causing significant disruption to daily life. The extreme weather event, which brought heavy rains, destructive winds, and widespread flooding, has prompted emergency responses and evacuation warnings across affected regions.

Storm Impact and Emergency Response

According to local authorities, wind speeds exceeding 120 km/h uprooted trees, damaged infrastructure, and caused road blockages. Power outages have been reported in several towns, with energy companies scrambling to restore services. Torrential rains have also led to flash flooding in low-lying areas, forcing families to evacuate their homes.

Emergency services have issued warnings urging residents to stay indoors and avoid unnecessary travel. Authorities have also deployed rescue teams to assist those stranded due to rising floodwaters. The Australian Bureau of Meteorology has advised that heavy rainfall and strong winds will persist for the next 24 hours, potentially worsening the situation.

Government Response and Climate Concerns
