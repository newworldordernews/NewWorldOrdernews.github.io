Tesla Faces Criticism Over Employee Sick Leave in Germany

Berlin, March 16, 2025 – Tesla is under fire in Germany after reports emerged that the company has refused to pay wages to employees on sick leave at its Gigafactory near Berlin. Employees and labor unions have raised concerns over Tesla's alleged practice of questioning medical leave certificates and withholding salaries, leading to growing outrage across the country.

Allegations of Unfair Labor Practices

Multiple workers at Tesla’s Brandenburg factory have reported instances where their sick leave was not recognized by the company. Some employees claim they were forced to provide additional proof of illness beyond official medical documentation, which is not required under German labor laws.

Labor unions, including IG Metall, have condemned Tesla’s policies, accusing the company of violating employee rights and creating a hostile work environment. “This is unacceptable behavior from a global corporation that claims to be a leader in innovation,” said a spokesperson from IG Metall. “Tesla must respect German labor laws and treat its workforce with fairness.”

Government and Public Response

The German Labor Ministry has stated that it is closely monitoring the situation and could take action if Tesla is found to be in violation of labor laws. Politicians have also weighed in, with some calling for stricter regulations on multinational companies operating in Germany.

Public backlash has been swift, with social media flooded with criticism of Tesla’s treatment of its workers. The hashtag #TeslaExploitsWorkers has been trending in Germany, and activists are calling for boycotts and protests outside the Gigafactory.

Tesla's Response

Tesla has denied any wrongdoing, claiming that all employee sick leave requests are reviewed fairly and according to German labor regulations. A company spokesperson stated, “We are committed to supporting our employees and ensuring that workplace policies align with legal requirements. Any misunderstandings are being addressed internally.”

What’s Next?

Labor unions have warned that they may escalate the situation by calling for strikes or legal action if Tesla does not change its policies. Meanwhile, German authorities may launch an investigation into the allegations, which could lead to potential fines or legal consequences for the automaker.

As the controversy unfolds, Tesla faces mounting pressure to uphold labor rights in one of Europe’s most regulated labor markets. The coming weeks will determine whether the company adjusts its policies or faces further backlash from workers, unions, and the German government.
